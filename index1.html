How to use this calculator

Enter your loan amount, annual interest rate, and term in years. Results update live as you type or drag the sliders — no button click needed. The full amortization schedule shows every payment month by month.

The loan payment formula

M = P × [r(1+r)ⁿ] / [(1+r)ⁿ − 1]

M = monthly payment  ·  P = principal  ·  r = monthly rate  ·  n = payments

Tips for a lower rate

Your credit score is the single biggest factor. A score above 750 typically unlocks the best rates. Compare at least 3 lenders and consider paying down existing debt before applying — even 1% less on a $20,000 loan saves over $600 in interest over 5 years.

FAQ

What is APR vs interest rate?
The interest rate is the cost of borrowing the principal. APR (Annual Percentage Rate) includes the rate plus fees like origination costs, giving a truer picture of the annual cost of the loan.
How do I lower my monthly payment?
Choose a longer term, secure a lower rate, or borrow less. Longer terms reduce monthly payments but increase total interest paid over the life of the loan.
Can I pay off my loan early?
Most personal loans allow early repayment. Some lenders charge a prepayment penalty — check your agreement. Extra principal payments each month reduce total interest significantly.
What credit score do I need?
Most lenders require a minimum score of 580–600. The best rates go to borrowers above 720–740. Credit unions often offer better rates than traditional banks for fair-credit borrowers.
Is this calculator accurate?
This uses the standard amortization formula with a fixed rate and no fees. For exact figures including APR and origination fees, always confirm directly with your lender.
What is an amortization schedule?
An amortization schedule is a table showing every payment over the loan's life — how much goes to principal vs interest each month, and the remaining balance after each payment.
