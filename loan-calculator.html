How to Use This Loan Calculator

Enter your loan amount, annual interest rate, and loan term in years. Select the loan type — most personal and car loans are amortizing, meaning each payment covers both principal and interest. Click Calculate Payment to see your monthly payment, total interest paid, and a full month-by-month amortization schedule.

What is an amortizing loan?

An amortizing loan is repaid through equal monthly payments over a fixed period. Early payments are mostly interest; as time goes on, a larger share goes toward the principal. By the end of the term, the balance reaches exactly zero.

Loan payment formula

M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n − 1]

M = monthly payment  |  P = principal  |  r = monthly rate  |  n = number of payments

For example: $10,000 loan at 7.5% for 5 years → monthly rate = 7.5%/12 = 0.625% → M = $200.38/month.

Tips for getting a lower interest rate

Your credit score is the single biggest factor in the rate you're offered. A score above 750 typically qualifies for the best rates. Paying down existing debt, avoiding new credit inquiries, and comparing at least 3 lenders can meaningfully reduce your rate — even 1% less on a $20,000 loan saves over $600 in interest over 5 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is APR vs interest rate?
The interest rate is the cost of borrowing the principal. APR (Annual Percentage Rate) includes the interest rate plus any fees (origination fees, closing costs), giving you a more accurate picture of the true annual cost of the loan.
How do I lower my monthly payment?
You can lower your monthly payment by choosing a longer loan term, securing a lower interest rate (improve your credit score or shop around), or borrowing a smaller amount. Keep in mind that a longer term means more total interest paid over the life of the loan.
Can I pay off my loan early?
Most personal loans allow early repayment. Some lenders charge a prepayment penalty — check your loan agreement. Paying extra toward principal each month reduces the total interest and shortens the loan term significantly.
What credit score do I need for a personal loan?
Most lenders require a minimum score of 580–600. The best rates are reserved for borrowers with scores above 720–740. Credit unions and online lenders sometimes offer better rates for borrowers with fair credit than traditional banks.
Is this calculator accurate?
This calculator uses the standard amortization formula and assumes a fixed interest rate with no additional fees. For an exact quote including origination fees and APR, always consult your lender directly.

💡 Quick Tips

🏦 Compare lenders — rates vary by 2–5% across banks, credit unions, and online lenders.
