How to Calculate Loan Payments

A loan payment is calculated using the standard amortization formula. The three inputs that determine your monthly payment are:

  1. Principal (P) — the amount you're borrowing
  2. Interest rate (r) — the annual rate divided by 12 to get the monthly rate
  3. Term (n) — the number of monthly payments (years × 12)

The loan payment formula

M = P × [r(1+r)ⁿ] / [(1+r)ⁿ − 1] Example: $20,000 at 7% for 5 years (60 months) Monthly rate r = 7% ÷ 12 = 0.5833% Monthly payment M = $396.02 Total repaid = $23,761 Total interest = $3,761

How to lower your monthly payment

  • Choose a longer repayment term (raises total interest paid)
  • Secure a lower interest rate (improve credit score, compare lenders)
  • Borrow less by increasing your down payment
  • Refinance an existing loan when rates drop

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this loan calculator require a signup or account?
No. LoanCalcHub is completely free with no account required. All calculators work instantly without registration — unlike many competitors that require you to create an account to see full results.
What is a loan amortization schedule?
An amortization schedule shows every payment over the loan's life, broken down into principal and interest. In early payments, more goes to interest. Over time, the balance shifts toward principal. LoanCalcHub generates the full table and lets you export it to CSV.
How accurate is this calculator?
The calculator uses the standard amortization formula with a fixed interest rate, which is how most installment loans work. Results are accurate for standard amortizing loans. Actual payments may differ slightly due to lender fees, rounding, or variable rates.
Can I compare two different loan scenarios?
Yes. The scenario comparison tool lets you compare your current loan parameters against an alternative — different rate, different term, or different amount — and shows the difference in monthly payment and total interest.
How do I calculate how much interest I'll save by paying extra each month?
Use the early payoff calculator tab. Enter your extra monthly payment amount and it instantly shows how much interest you save and how many months earlier you'll be debt-free.
